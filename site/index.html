Why teams keep it open

Three reasons it earns a permanent place in your workflow.

The landing page should be simple because the value proposition is simple: QuickPreview helps you find moments, return to context, and keep sensitive material protected.

01

A bookmark manager you will actually rely on

Save exact moments into a sortable, searchable library with thumbnail previews, tags, and direct media imports so your references stay useful long after the first review session.

02

Every clip keeps its own setup

QuickPreview remembers the state that matters for each clip, including screen position, window size, playback position, time range, sound, and other review context, so reopening feels instant instead of repetitive.

03

Privacy with a second lock when you need it

Protected media can require macOS authentication and an optional second password layer, giving sensitive material a deliberate barrier instead of wishful privacy.

What supports the core promise

Drag and drop clips, switch directly from Finder, move through bookmarks with the arrow keys, use Shift plus the arrows when you need frame stepping, rotate awkward footage, boost quiet audio, and close the window instantly when you are done.

Step 01

Open a clip without breaking your flow

Start from File Open, drag and drop, Finder selection, or the optional background shortcut when you want QuickPreview ready in seconds.

Step 02

Scrub, seek, switch bookmarks, and loop with intent

QuickPreview gives you the control to move fast when scanning and slow down exactly when the clip deserves a closer look, without breaking bookmark flow.

Step 03

Save the moment and come back to it properly

Capture the bookmark, return later to the same clip state, and keep sensitive material behind the level of protection the moment deserves.
